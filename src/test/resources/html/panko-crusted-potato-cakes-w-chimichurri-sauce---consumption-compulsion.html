Panko Crusted Potato Cakes w/ Chimichurri Sauce

April 4, 2015 By Consumption Compulsion 6 Comments

Total Time: 30 minutes   Yield: 15 Cakes    Level: Easy

These spicy panko crusted potato cakes are perfectly crisp on the outside, yet smooth and creamy on the inside.  Each cake is chock full of cilantro, mint, green onions, and green chilis giving each bite a burst of intense flavors (and quite a kick too!).This recipe is basically our take on the Indian “Aloo tikki/ cutlets”  which we grew up with and loved. However, instead of using bread crumbs we decided to use panko because their larger flakes tend to remain crispier longer than breadcrumbs.

This time around we are also providing a short video of how to prepare this delicious and super-easy dish.  We’ve also launched a  new Youtube channel so please subscribe and look out for more video recipes!Instead of pairing this dish with a traditional indian cilantro chutney we decided to make a spicy chimichurri sauce; which suprisingly turned out perfectly.You can have them as a side with your favorite meal or just by themselves with afternoon tea. We also love eating them with boiled rice and spicy lentil curry.I can’t wait for you guys to try this recipe out and as always please let us know how the recipe went and share your pics.

Enjoy!

Panko Crusted Potato Cakes w/ Chimichurri Sauce
 
Save Print
Prep time
15 mins
Cook time
15 mins
Total time
30 mins
 
These spicy panko crusted potato cakes are perfectly crisp on the outside, yet smooth and creamy on the inside.
Author: Consumption Compulsion
Yield: 15 cakes
Ingredients
Potato Cakes
  • Potatoes – 7 (large) or 2.5 lbs
  • Cilantro Chopped – 1 heaping cup
  • Fresh Mint Chopped – ¾ cup
  • Green Onions Finely Sliced – 1 cup
  • Green Chilies Finely Chopped – 2 Tbsp
  • Lemon Juice – 1 Lemon
  • Salt – 1 Tbsp
  • Red Chili Powder – 1 tsp
  • Eggs – 2
  • Milk – 1 Tbsp
  • Panko Bread Crumbs – 2 to 3 cups
  • Oil for frying
Chimichurri Sauce
  • Fresh Cilantro – 1 cup
  • Fresh Italian Parsley – 1 cup
  • Fresh Oregano Leaves – 1 Tbsp
  • Fresh Hot Green Chilies – 4
  • Garlic – 2 Cloves
  • Cumin Powder – ½ tsp
  • Red Pepper Flakes – ½ tsp
  • Salt – 1 tsp
  • Vinegar – 3 Tbsp
  • Olive Oil – 4 tsp
Instructions
Potato Cakes
  1. Peel and cut potatoes in 1-inch cubes and boil them.
  2. In a bowl, mash potatoes with salt, red chili powder and lemon juice.
  3. Now add cilantro, mint, green chilies and green onions and mix.
  4. Shape into ½ inch thick patties.
  5. Prepare a dipping station, with eggs (beaten with milk) in one bowl and panko bread crumbs in another.
  6. Coat the cakes with egg mixture then coat them generously with panko bread crumbs.
  7. Shallow fry your cakes until crispy and golden brown.
Chimichurri Sauce
  1. Put all the above chimichurri sauce ingredients in a blender and blend! blend! blend! Until smooth and thick, with a tomato sauce like consistency.
Notes
Don’t add extra vinegar or olive oil thinking there’s not enough liquid. Be patient and it’ll all blend nicely. You can help mix it in between pulses with a spatula.
